Abstract 🌱🌈: This investigation explores, under what color light mung beans grow the tallest and most effectively in a week. For the set-up, we used boxes that have transparent film attached to them, coloured PVC films, part of a card board box for the black light box, petri dish, tissues, and mung beans. We took out the transparent film which was already on the box and stuck the trimmed color film. And then, we put 4 mung beans on the wet tissue. Collected data and results showed that Black light showed the most dramatic growth, from day 2 to day 4. White, yellow, green, red, blue light showed the most dramatic growth from day 4 to day 7. The result showed that initially, mung beans grown in no light grew the tallest. However, towards the end of the experiment, mung beans grown in red light proved to have grown the tallest. On the other hand, the shortest mung beans were the mung beans grown in yellow light, but at the end of the experiment, the shortest mung beans were the mung beans grown in green light. The result showed the black light was first, red light was second, blue light was third, white light was fourth, green light was fifth, and yellow light was last at the start of the experiment. At the end of the experiment, the first one was red, second one was blue, third one was black, fourth one was white, fifth one was yellow, last one was green.

Hypothesis 💫: If mung beans are grown under blue and red light, then they will show the greatest increase in height and number of leaves over 7 days, compared to plants grown under green, yellow, white, and black light. This is because blue and red wavelengths are absorbed most effectively by chlorophyll and are therefore the most efficient for photosynthesis and growth.

Independent variable 💚: The colour of light the plants are exposed to (blue, red, green, yellow, white, and black)

Dependent variable 🪴:
Plant growth, measured by:
Height of the mung bean plants (cm)
Number of leaves produced

Control variable ✨:
Amount of water given to each plant (10 mL per day)
Type of plant (mung bean seeds from the same source)
Growing medium (same type and amount of paper towel)
Number of seeds in each petri dish (5 seeds)
Size and type of container (identical petri dishes)
Light exposure time each day (same duration for all groups)
Distance from the light source (boxes placed next to each other)
Temperature and environment (same windowsill location)
Ventilation (same number of holes in each box)
Duration of the experiment (7 days)
Measurement method (same ruler and measuring technique)

Introduction & Purpose 🍃:
Plants require light, water, carbon dioxide, and nutrients to grow and carry out photosynthesis, a process where light energy is converted into glucose, which provides energy for growth. Light is made up of different colours, each with different wavelengths, and these colours can affect how efficiently photosynthesis occurs. Chlorophyll, the green pigment found in chloroplasts, absorbs light most effectively in the red and blue regions of the light spectrum, while green light is mostly reflected, making it less effective for plant growth. This means that different colours of light may have different impacts on how well a plant grows and develops. This investigation aims to explore how different colours of light (blue, red, green, yellow, white, and black) affect the growth of mung bean plants over a period of 7 days. Growth will be measured by recording the height of the plants under each light condition. By keeping all other variables the same, this experiment ensures that any differences in growth are due to the colour of light. This investigation is important because it helps improve our understanding of how light influences photosynthesis and plant growth. This knowledge can be applied in real-life situations such as agriculture, greenhouse growing, and plant care, where controlling light conditions can help maximise plant growth and productivity. It also demonstrates how scientific experiments are designed by controlling variables and testing one factor at a time to produce reliable and accurate results.
