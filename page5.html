Conclusion 🌿: In conclusion, the results of this investigation show that the colour of light affects the growth of mung bean plants. Over the 7-day period, the plants increased in height from approximately 0.5 cm on day 1 to 1.5 cm on day 2, reaching around 3.2 cm by day 4, and 5.1 cm on day 7. This steady and rapid growth shows that the conditions were suitable for germination and early development.

Overall, the results matched the hypothesis that mung beans grown under blue and red light would show the greatest increase in height and leaf development. The plants under blue light showed the most growth, with red light also producing strong results. This supports the idea that blue and red wavelengths are absorbed most effectively by chlorophyll, making them the most efficient for photosynthesis and growth. However, it was quite unexpected that the plants grown in no light (black) were still able to grow to some extent. This can be explained by the fact that seeds use stored energy during the early stages of germination, allowing initial growth even without light. Despite this, these plants would not be able to keep growing healthily in the long term.

Overall, this investigation supports scientific understanding of photosynthesis and highlights the importance of light colour in plant growth.
